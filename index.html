A Reflection on Education, Language, and Cultural Adaptation

Introduction

Hey, I'm Owais. This project is about my journey to Canada and how I adapted to a new culture and school system.

I came to Canada about three years ago when I was 16. Since then, I've picked up English, gotten used to a different way of learning, and found some cool opportunities for my future.

My Journey to Canada

My family left Turkey because school was really tough there. I struggled with Turkish and it made learning everything else super hard.

Coming to Canada felt like a fresh start. I was actually excited to learn English since I always thought it was cool and useful worldwide.

Moving here gave me hope for a better future, and I was ready for new opportunities.

Learning English

Before Canada, my English was pretty basic - just a few words I picked up in Syria and from playing Minecraft.

I got better fast by talking with friends who don't speak Arabic, working as a cashier, and just being in school.

Learning English wasn't that hard for me because I actually enjoyed it. I'm pretty happy with how much I've improved, but I still want to get even better.

School Life: Then and Now

Back in Syria, I did great in grades 1-6, but Turkey was tough. I had to repeat grade 7, did grade 8 online during COVID, and really struggled in grade 9 before we moved here.

Canadian schools are way easier and more supportive. The teachers and other kids are nice, especially to newcomers. We only have four classes each day with the same schedule, which helps me stay focused.

I really like how sports matter here. In Syria and Turkey, we just had one PE class a week, but here it's actually important - you even need it to graduate!

Cultural Differences

Canada is really different from Syria and Turkey - the food, traditions, and just everyday life aren't the same at all.

School discipline is totally different too. In Syria, teachers could hit students for small mistakes, but that's illegal here. Teachers actually respect students in Canada.

The best thing about living here is knowing I can build a good future. There are tons of programs and opportunities to find a career I actually enjoy.

Personal Growth

Since coming to Canada, I've grown a lot. I'm way more confident now that I can speak English, and I've figured out how to juggle work, school, and my personal life.

I've also found some new things I love, like soccer and working out. I play soccer all the time now and I'm trying to get a six-pack!

This whole experience has taught me how to be tough and adapt to change - skills that'll definitely help me later in life.

Growth happens outside your comfort zone.

Future Goals

I'm not totally sure what I want to do after high school yet, but I've got two main ideas: becoming a civil engineer or working with cars - painting and mechanics.

Civil engineering would be tough and costs a lot, but I'm really into it. Car work is also cool because I love cars and enjoy working with my hands.

Whatever I end up doing, I know coming to Canada has given me chances I never would've had before.

Conclusion

Coming to Canada was one of the best things my family ever did. It gave me another shot at doing well in school, learning a new language, and building a better future.

I'm really thankful for all the opportunities I've found here, and I'm excited to keep growing and reaching my goals.
